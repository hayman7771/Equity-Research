# 3-Minute Deal Screen – Midwest Industrial Solutions (“MIS”)

## 1. Basic Facts
- **Company:** Midwest Industrial Solutions (“MIS”)
- **Sector:** Specialty industrial components / engineered parts
- **Geography:** Midwest U.S., serving North American OEMs
- **Ownership situation:** Founder-owned, exploring liquidity/transition options
- **Size:** ~\ Revenue / ~\.2M EBITDA (TTM)

## 2. Quick Thesis
**Interesting because:**  
- Niche engineered products with high switching costs and recurring orders.  
- Healthy EBITDA, strong FCF conversion, and visible operational improvement levers.  

**Main concern:**  
- Customer concentration (top 3 at ~40% of revenue). Need to understand contract structures, churn history, and insourcing risk.

## 3. Initial Financial View
- **Revenue growth:** ~6–7% CAGR over last 3 years  
- **EBITDA margin:** ~14% (below best-in-class industrial peers)  
- **FCF conversion:** ~80–85% of EBITDA (capex-light, moderate working capital)  
- **Capex intensity:** ~2–3% of sales  
- **Leverage (contemplated):** 3.5–4.0x EBITDA, comfortable for stable industrial cash flows  

## 4. Industry View
- **Fragmentation:** Industry remains fragmented with many sub-scale regional competitors.  
- **Growth vs mature:** Modest GDP+ growth; defensive demand given components are mission-critical for OEM customers.  
- **Secular drivers:** Reshoring, automation, and demand for reliable supply chains support steady volumes.  

## 5. Next Step?
**Decision:** DIG DEEPER  

**Focus areas:**  
- Customer concentration risk (contract terms, switching history, share of wallet)  
- Operational diagnostics (throughput, scheduling, procurement practices)  
- Margin expansion feasibility (gap vs peers, specific initiatives, required capex)  
- M&A pipeline to support a broader “buy-and-build” platform thesis.
